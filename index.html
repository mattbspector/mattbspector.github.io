Lesean Mccoy

Running Back | Philidelphia Eagles | 5'11" | 208lb | Experience: 6 years

Why You Should Draft Him

2013 was a career high year for Lesean Mccoy. He set career highs with 314 carries and 1,607 rushing yards. He is also considered a king of the PPR throne, having more than 50 catches in three of the last four seasons. Shady truly benefits from a Chip Kelly run-heavy offense, and a possible 2000 yard season does not seem out of reach. Look for Mccoy to have another impressive season as he rules the one of the many PPR kingdoms.

Why You Should Not Draft Him

Having a less impressive season than his 2013 season is almost inevitable. It is extremely likely that his Rushing Yards per game will decrease, and his opportunities to catch passes from the backfield might regress as well. The Eagles brought in PPR monster Darren Sproles this offseason, and he is expected to take away some of Mccoy's reps. However, Sproles should not be the reason you do not draft McCoy. Just look for a slight regression this year compared to his 2013 campaign.

Three Facts

1.  He has put up 200+ Fantasy Points (Yahoo! Standard Scoring) in three of the last four years.

2.  He is only a year removed from an injury that limited him to 12 games and 5 touchdowns.

3.  The last time a player led the league in rushing two years in a row was LaDainian Tomlinson in 2006-2007.

Adrian Peterson

Running Back | Minnesota Vikings | 6'1" | 217lb | Experience: 8 years

Why You Should Draft Him

You know what they say? Purple is the color or royalty. You know what they call Peterson? Purple Jesus. Peterson surprised nobody last year, averaging 90 rushing yards a game. Rumor has it that Peterson has been working on his pass-catching skills, and with Gerhart out of the picture, look for Peterson's PPR value to rise. Norv Turner also plans on using AP in a more creative way, getting him more involved in open space and in the passing the game. Peterson should definitely be looked at as a high end first round pick.

Why You Should Not Draft Him

There are many questions surrounding whether or not AP is going to ask to leave the Vikings this year, as he has expressed a good amount of unhappiness with the quarterback situation and may request to take his talents elsewhere. He is also close to turning 30 and past the average age of elite Running Backs. Lastly, look out for his injury risk. His body has certainly taken a toll at this point in his career, and his way of running through people makes him a likely candidate for a possible injury.

Three Facts

1.  Shaun Alexander, Ladainanian Tomlinson, and Michael Turner all declined drastically after they were 29 years old.

2.  "All Day" has had seven straight seasons of double digit touchdowns.

3.  Under Norv Turner the Chargers running backs accounted for 112 receptions in 2013.

Jimmy Graham

Tight End | New Orleans Saints | 6'7" | 265lb | Experience: 5 years

Why You Should Draft Him

Over the course of the last three years, Graham has hands-down been the best tight end in football. With the departures of Darren Sproles and Lance Moore through free agency, expect Graham to be targeted even more often in the high-powered Saints offense. Last year, he scored the second most touchdowns ever for a tight end. With more potential targets and a proven history, Jimmy Graham will be a hard to pass up.

Why You Should Not Draft Him

In his final five games, including the playoffs, Graham was held to only 2 touchdowns. While some people attribute it to defenses discovering a way to slow him down, most people believe that Jimmy Graham was actually bothered by a plantar fascia injury. With what is expected to be an enormous workload this season, let us all hope that the injury bug doesn't rear its ugly head.

Three Facts

1.  Jimmy Graham was targeted 144 times last season, 25 more targets than any other tight end.

2.  "All Day"Over the last three seasons, Graham has accounted for 36 touchdowns and 3507 receiving yards.

3.  In his final 7 games last year, Graham never exceeded 72 receiving yards.
