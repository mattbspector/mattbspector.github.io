Why You Should Draft Him

2013 was a career high year for Lesean Mccoy. He set career highs with 314 carries and 1,607 rushing yards. He is also considered a king of the PPR throne, having more than 50 catches in three of the last four seasons. Shady truly benefits from a Chip Kelly run-heavy offense, and a possible 2000 yard season does not seem out of reach. The Eagles brought in PPR monster Darren Sproles this offseason, and he is expected to take away some of Mccoy's reps. However, Sproles should not be the reason you do not draft McCoy. Just look for a slight regression this year compared to his 2013 campaign.

Three Facts

1.  He has put up 200+ Fantasy Points (Yahoo! Standard Scoring) in three of the last four years.

2.  He is only a year removed from an injury that limited him to 12 games and 5 touchdowns.

3.  The last time a player led the league in rushing two years in a row was LaDainian Tomlinson in 2006-2007.

Matt Forte

Running Back | Chicago Bears | 6'2" | 218lb | Experience: 7 years | Bye Week: 9

Why You Should Draft Him

Two years ago, Forte would have been considered a prince, waiting to rule the throne. Well the time has come people! Forte is not just a PPR king, he is a Fantasy King. He put up career numbers last year, averaging 23 touches per game and finishing the year with 1,339 total rushing yards. Also, his fantasy value in PPR leagues has no ceiling. Pick him early with confidence, in all formats, as he could easily finish the season as the best fantasy running back.

Why You Should Not Draft Him

Forte has had serious injury concerns in the past, and after another year of playing in the NFL, his chances of re-injury are higher. Also, The Chicago Bears offense now features two elite receivers in Brandon Marshall and Alshon Jeffery. Although this did not affect Forte's performance a season ago, there is potential that the Bears will focus on getting the ball to their two standout wideouts more often.

Three Facts

1.  In Marc Trestman's first year as the Bears Head Coach, Forte posted a career best rushing yards (1,339), career best rushing touchdowns (9), career best receptions (74), and career best receiving yards (594).

2.  Forte lead all running backs in playing time, participating in 87.8% of the Bears snaps. In second, was Lesean McCoy with 79.1% of the Eagles total snaps.

3.   Forte finished in the top 30 in total receptions, finishing with more catches than Wes Welker, Mike Wallace, Victor Cruz, and Greg Jennings.

Aaron Rodgers

Quarterback | Green Bay Packers | 6'2" | 225lb | Experience: 10 years

Why You Should Draft Him

Aaron Rodgers has a skillset unlike any other quarterback in the NFL. Rodgers pairs elite accuracy and arm strength with a superb running ability. Although he missed some time last year with a collarbone injury, Rodgers managed to throw for at least 240 yards and a touchdown in every game he started. With the emergence of Eddie Lacy as an offensive threat, expect Rodgers to have more room to operate and shred opposing defenses.

Why You Should Not Draft Him

One major worry regarding Aaron Rodgers is the very shaky offensive line play of the Green Bay Packers. Over the last two seasons, the Green Bay Packers have allowed an astounding 97 sacks. Anybody willing to take Aaron Rodgers early in their fantasy draft better hope that the offensive line can keep him on his feet so he doesn't suffer the same fate he did last season missing 7 games.

Three Facts

1.  Aaron Rodgers holds the highest career regular season passer rating in NFL history.

2.  In 6 years as an NFL starter, Rodgers has accounted for 187 passing touchdowns and only 51 interceptions.

3.  Aaron Rodgers was the first quarterback to throw for 4,000 yards in his first two seasons as a starter.
